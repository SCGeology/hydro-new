Streamflow Data

The U.S. Geological Survey operates and maintains a network of real-time stream gages across the State. The SCDNR currently supports funding for 16 of these gages. Data from these sites are used to support drought assessments, hydrologic modeling and water planning activities in the State.

The following telephone numbers provide lake level information.

Santee Cooper Lakes (Marion and Moultrie)
1-800-925-2537
Santee-Cooper's Lakes Information Line offers lake levels for Lakes Marion and Moultrie, as well as inflow and discharge rates. The information is updated daily at 10:00 am.

Duke Energy Lakes (Jocassee, Keowee, Wateree, Wylie, and Fishing Creek)
1-800-829-5253
The Duke Energy Lake Information Line provides up-to-the-minute water levels for Lakes Jocassee and Keowee; Lake Wylie (Middle Catawba); and Lakes Wateree and Fishing Creek (both Lower Catawba), as well as some other lakes in North Carolina. This phone number does not provide actual water surface elevations; rather, it provides lake levels relative to the full pond level, which is assumed to be 100.0 feet. For example, if the level of Lake Wylie is given as 97.6 feet, the lake would be 2.4 feet below its level when full.
