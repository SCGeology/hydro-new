Overview

     Projections of future water use are critical to the planning process. In order to develop State and regional plans that effectively balance the economic, environmental and social needs of South Carolina for generations to come, estimates of future water demand are needed.

     In 2018, the SCDNR, in cooperation with the United States’ Army Corps of Engineers and the Clemson University’s South Carolina Water Resource Center, began developing water demand projection methodologies to apply statewide. The SCDNR organized a Technical Advisory Committee to inform the development of the projection methodologies. More information about the process for developing water demand projection methodologies is found below.

     Water demand projections will be completed for a 50-year planning horizon for the Energy, Public Water Supply, Industry, Golf Course, and Agricultural Irrigation water-use sectors. Water users and stakeholder in each of the eight river planning basins, will have the opportunity to review and comment on draft water demand projections.
