Overview

     The South Carolina Department of Natural Resource (DNR) is legislatively mandated through the South Carolina Water Resources Planning and Coordination Act (§ 49-3-10, et. seq., Code of Laws of South Carolina, 1976) to formulate and establish a comprehensive water resources policy for the State. The first edition of the South Carolina Water Plan was published in 1998 (Cherry and Badr, 1998). The plan was subsequently updated in 2004 (Badr and others, 2004) incorporating experience and knowledge gained from the severe, statewide drought of 1998–2002. The 2004 Water Plan highlighted the need for water planning on a regional level and in 2014 the SCDNR initiated the first steps to develop regional or river basin water plans that will ultimately support the development of a new State Water Plan. The regional plans, or River Basin Plans (RBPs), will developed for the eight major river basins in the State – Broad, Catawba, Edisto, Pee Dee, Salkahatchie, Saluda, Santee, and Savannah.
